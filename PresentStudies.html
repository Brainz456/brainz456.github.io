Present Studies

As of September 2015, I am currently studying a degree in Computer Science with a Year in Industry at Cardiff University. My expected graduation date shall be 2019, by which point I will have knowledge of a multitude of programming languages such as Python, Java and C++ along with an in-depth knowledge of topics that will help in the career I wish to enter when I finish. My aspirations after completing my bachelor’s degree would be to continue my education further and proceed towards a master’s degree while also looking to work within the industry, specifically within network security.

During my first semester I have already gained experience in working as a group to design and program a text-based game within the space of one week. During this task I took the role of a leader within the group and worked on a way to designate roles between us so that we could produce the best quality game. The game was finally coded in Python with the main code work split between myself and two others, while other members of the team worked on debugging, story and presentation elements. I was particularly pleased with the way that I wrote the code for the Blackjack mini-game within our project (which can be found here) as it was a task I took on myself, having not a large amount of prior experience with the language, and I feel that it the code was executed quite well, though on reflection some changes could be made for efficiency etc.

Another project I have worked on for some of the semester is my Professional Skills LaTeX coursework. The brief was to reproduce a pdf document that would be the same as the one provided along with the brief. To do this, I researched many different packages that would allow me to recreate the pdf as best as I possibly could. The main complication of the project was trying to find packages that would work as closely as possible to the original document without having any knowledge of the packages that were used for the example. Overall I feel that my submission for this task was quite accurate in its reproduction and made good use of documentation and packages that I could find in order to complete the task.

During my first year of university, I have been studying Japanese as an extracurricular activity through the universities “Languages For All” scheme. Part of the reason for learning the language is that I would like the opportunity to travel to Japan after my degree, possibly as some form of internship or work experience. My plan is to complete the language course to such a level that I can communicate with contacts should the need arise. I feel that my skills with the language will develop throughout my degree to reach my goals and will provide me another qualification to add my portfolio.

Finally, I have recently started looking into learning few programming languages via online tutorial websites such as CodeAcademy. I feel that extending my knowledge to further programming languages will help me both learn a new aspect of development for computers as well as giving me another aspect to add to my list of skills within a work approach. My first focus will be SQL as I can learn the basics of database and server interaction, which will help further me towards my aspiration of working within the networking industry after my degree.
