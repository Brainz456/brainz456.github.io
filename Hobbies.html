Hobbies

One of my biggest hobbies that I have is volunteering at an official Pokemon League for the the trading card game. I started as a competitor two and a half years ago and after reaching the age of 18, signed up to the volunteer “Professor Program” that allowed me to give back to the league I started at while also gaining experience in responsibility of others including setting up accounts to connect with the Organised Play system. Now I help with managing the League, helping the running of some tournaments and next season I hope to act as a judge in the regional tournament that may be hosted in Cardiff.

Another hobby of mine, which I have mentioned in my Present Studies, is the learning of Japanese. While I have talked about how this may help me in future work, I feel that the language study should also be regarded as a hobby as my main goal with learning another language was to be able to travel to another country and have some knowledge of the language in order to converse and navigate the country easier than without the skills. Secondly, learning a new language could potentially help me in career aspects either by looking prosperous by employers or by allowing for more connections to networks of people as it allows more effective communication between myself and someone else.

Over the years, gaming has been a passionate hobby for me since my introduction to video games as a child. This can be seen by my inclusion in the Cardiff University Gaming Society, which I attend semi-regularly and my time playing video games specifically DOTA. Over the years of playing multiplayer games, I have improved skills such as communication between teams; teamwork and decision making as well as making a strong group of friends that can work together efficiently. I feel these developments have contributed to my ability to work within teams at a professional level as well as contributing to my ability to lead a group on a task, such as our group project during the first semester of university.

As a lot of my work revolves around desk work, I try to keep healthy but attending the gym regularly. While at the gym, I usually work with weights for the majority of my time there before spending some time working on cardiovascular activity to stay in shape. Usage of the weights has helped improve my focus of a task over the years and helps me to maintain a healthy lifestyle for myself.

Something that I have done a few times in the past and something that I would love to be able to do after graduation via my job would be to travel. I have been to quite a few countries throughout my life including my most recent trip to Iceland three years ago, as part of a study trip with my School at the time. The trip itself was fantastic, as it brought together two different G.C.S.E qualifications and showcased them both as we looked into different areas of Iceland's landscape. I had the great oppurtunity to see Eyjafjallajökull, the volcano that caused the major air services issues for the majority of Europe in 2010. The overall trip was unforgettable and given the oppurtunity, I would be more than happy to visit again. In the future, I think that my main ambition of travel would be focused towards East Asia, specifically Japan and South Korea.
